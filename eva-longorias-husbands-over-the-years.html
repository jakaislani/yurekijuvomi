Inside actress Eva Longoria's many marriages: The 'Young and Restless' actress is a mother to one!  

Eva Longoria's multi-faceted career has been a giant force in Hollywood. From foraying into the intimidating acting world with no connection in the 90s to anchoring a career as a director, and producer, Eva has come a long way. Born on March 15, 1975, in Corpus Christi, Texas, Eva has Mexican roots.  

Longoria's stellar career has, in fact, been a monumental establishment for aspiring Latinos actors in Hollywood. She landed her big break, playing Gabrielle Solis in ABC's successful series, Desperate Housewives, in 2004. The actress's prolific resume also includes roles in The Young and the Restless, Frontera, and Lowriders.  

The Truth About Eva Longoria's Failed Marriages!  

In search of fame and fortune, Eva met and fell in love with a number of men; some of those romances were well publicized, while a few ran under the radar. The Golden Globe-winning actress has been married three times and divorced twice. Eva was in her mid-20s when she got hitched to actor Tyler Christopher, in 2002.  

Longoria's nuptial with the fellow soap opera star, best known for his roles in General Hospital, and Days of Our Lives, seemed perfect on the surface, with the pair following similar career trajectories, and keeping it low at all times. But the marriage lasted less than two years as they called it quits in 2004.  

The Desperate Housewives actress later cited her age and shortcomings as a wife for the failure of their marriage. "He was a lovely man, but I was very young," the actress explained, adding, "I didn't know who I was, and I was still struggling as an actor. I was a terrible wife."   

Christopher went on to marry his second wife, Brienne Pedigo, in 2008, and as for Longoria, she found love again with Tony Parker, a former basketball player. The actress and Parker started dating almost immediately after Longoria split ways with Tyler.   

The love birds enjoyed a two-year-long dating bliss before announcing their engagement in November 2006 and getting married almost a year later in July 2007. Longoria's second divorce was a bitter experience for the actress as it ended because of Parker's infidelity.   

The couple- once loved for being the biggest cheerleaders of each other, were over by the year 2010. While the initial reason was cited as "irreconcilable differences," it was later revealed that the soap opera actress had discovered hundreds of messages from a woman on Parker's phone.  

The shocking cheating scandal also unveiled Parker's mistress. The other woman was said to be Parker's then-fellow teammate, Brent Barry's wife, Erin Barry. Longoria admitted to struggling with her self-esteem amid the cheating scandal. And Parker, on his part, also expressed remorse over their failed marriage.   

But having faced two failed nuptials and a few unnoticed relationships, the Desperate Housewives actress doesn't look on the experience with regret. Longoria believes that all those relationships came into her life for all the right reasons. "I think every relationship is a path to where you are today," said the optimistic actress.
